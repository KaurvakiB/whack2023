Generalized Anxiety Disorder Screening

Generalized Anxiety Disorder is characterized by persistent, uncontrollable worrying, especially for days on end, recurring over at least 6 months (Johns Hopkins).
College students are experiencing increasingly high levels of reported anxiety, among other mental health struggles, with 37% reporting anxiety in the 2021-2022 school year in the Healthy Minds Survey, as reported by Michigan News.

The following questions follow the process for Generalized Anxiety Disorder Screening, based on the Primary Care Evaluation of Mental Disorders Patient Health Questionaire (also known as the PRIME-MD-PHQ, or just PHQ for short).
The evaluation is based off of your responses to the following series of questions, calculating a final score.
The following detail the points associated with each response:
Not at all = 1
Several days = 2
More than half the days = 3
Nearly every day = 4
A scale is used to determine the evaluation based on the sum of all points from each question:
0-4: minimal anxiety
5-9: mild anxiety
10-14: moderate anxiety
15-21: severe anxiety
